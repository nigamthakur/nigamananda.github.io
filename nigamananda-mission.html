Inception

Sadguru Paramahansa Paribrajakacharya Sri 108 Swami Nigamananda Saraswatidev, only the master of four-fold spiritual disciplines i.e. Yoga, Jnana, Tantra, Prema, founded “Gouranga Anath Niketan”, which was known later on as “Gouranga Sevashrama” at “Santi Ashram” on 26th Agrahayan 1318 B.S. at dacca with the support of Dr. Rajchandra Dhar and it was dedicated to the name of Sri Sri Gouranga Mahaprabhu, glory of Nadia District. His main motto and objectives was to alleviate the miseries of the distressed people, to arrange food for helpless boys and girls, to arrange free medical treatment for the ailing countrymen, to arrange money, clothing food etc for the calamity-stricken people during drought, flood, earthquake etc.

He sent his dear dedicated workers in 1318 at Assam during tremendous famine at cachar district, during unexpected flood of Damodar in 1319 B.S. at the six districts in south Bengal and arrangement of free medical treatment for the people who suffered from pneumonia, cholera etc to arrange necessary help to the flood-victim people at Midnapore and Orissa in 1320 B.S. During flood at Burdawan Section, during severe famine thought-out Bengal, during flopd at north and south Bengal in 1326 B.S. and 1338 B.S. during devasting earthquake in 1340 B.S. at Bihar and also his dedicated teams render necessary help and services to the people affected during the flood at 1341 B.S. in east Bengal. Sri Sri Thakur Maharaj left his mortal body in 1342 B.S. and entered into Mahasamadhi. In order to give a concrete shape of his dream towards Service and relief work, Sri Sri Nigamananda Charitable Dispensary has been established at Assam-Bangiya Saraswat Math in 1384 B.S. and Sri Sri Nigamananda Relief Committee in 1395 B.S.

Growth

Under these two sections, about 2000 patients have been undergone cataract operations, testing of eyes of about 13000 patients, necessary assistance during flood at jalpaiguri and Coochbihar districts, during conflagration at baripada, during Tornado at dantan, during inundation of river at bongaon, flood in malda, super cyclone at orissa, during flood at north 24 paraganas, hooghly, nadia etc under south Bengal, flood at east midnapur district, inundation at south 24 paraganas and later providing food, clothing, hpuse-hold equipments, free medical treatments with supply of medicines to the lakh of people affected by the devastating AILA storms. In our allopathic and homeopathic sections under charitable dispensary, 21 experienced doctors render voluntary services towards about 1000 patients in a year. In order to make our efforts more effective and extensive towards the affected people, we have founded “NIGAMANANDA MISSION, HALISAHAR” which has also been notified in Govt. record.
